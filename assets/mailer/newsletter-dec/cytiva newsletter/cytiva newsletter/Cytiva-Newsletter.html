We erroneously believe that harassment is always sexual. Harassment can be of any kind - mental, physical, visual, verbal, or written. This is my story of being mentally and emotionally harassed at the workplace and how I reported it.

In my role as a consultant, I was reporting to two senior leaders, who had been with the organization for 20 years. Soon after, one of them started placing unprofessional demands like purchase. of out-of-contract and high-end water bottles for personal use. conversion of personal room rates to corporate rates during family vacation, and other personal demands, to name a few instances. Often, I was compelled to behave unprofessionally towards internal and external stakeholders. I was berated for being incapable if I refused to fulfill non-compliant demands,

It had a huge impact on my mental health, confidence, and self-esteem, which reflected in my professional and personal life. As I was new to the organization, I felt incompetent to deal with the senior and hesitant to report to my reporting manager.

I dealt with this ordeal for over 7 months before I gathered the courage to address it with the HR manager. Yet I felt hesitant to report the incidents in fear of retaliation and humiliation.

The Danaher Code of Conduct underlines our commitment to Diversity and Inclusion and workplace free of harassment.,

A week later, I formally reported the unprofessional incidents to the HR manager, who then reported the same to the AVP-HR. A meeting was scheduled with the offender.

The AVP -HR personally expressed regret for what I had to go through and gratitude for bringing the incidents to light. For the first time I felt safe, valued, and respected, and my journey has continued in the same spirit for the past 5 years. Subsequent requests from the senior have been professional and in line with the JD.

At the outset, had I had the courage to directly confront the senior about the inappropriate. behavior or report to the manager, this ordeal would have been quickly over. I have since learned not to tolerate any kind of harassment and bullying, and encourage my colleagues (of any gender) to voice their concerns of similar manner.

Whether you are an employee or a consultant, whether the harassment occurs at the workplace or at a customer location, please know that our internal committee is here to listen to and support in every way possible with the sole purpose of making this company a healthy and safe place.

Yours sincerely,

Kavitha Subramaniam
